• • Saturn is, at their closest distance, approximately 1.2 billion km (roughly 7 AU) from Earth.
  • • There is no definite time on how long it takes to get to Saturn. How fast and how long it takes to travel to Saturn depends on the method used to get there.
  • • There are two methods used to propel a spacecraft to Saturn: either using the gravity of another planet to ”slingshot” the ship to Saturn, or directly going there.
  • • Pioneer 11 took 6 and a half years to arrive. Voyager 1 took 3 years and 2 months, Voyager 2 took four years, and the Cassini spacecraft took six years and nine months to arrive. The New Horizons spacecraft took a short two years and four months to arrive.
  • • On average, it takes approximately 4 to 6 years to arrive at Saturn, depending on the method used, the engines used, the spacecraft used,  Saturn’s distance from the sun, and whether there will be flyovers with other planets.
