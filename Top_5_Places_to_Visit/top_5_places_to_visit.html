1. Titan

Titan is the most similar body to Earth. It is the only body which has the an atmosphere. When the Huygens lander of the ESA-NASA joint mission Casinni-Huygens landed on Titan. It was believed that it had a surface of methane. It had supercooled volcanoes. There are also lakes of methane.

2. Enceladus - Plumes

Enceladus is one of the top two places to visit when you go to Saturn.

It is a magnificent moon. What makes Enceladus so unique is because of 2 main features: The Tiger Stripes and The Southern Plumes.  The Southern Plumes is located towards the south of the moon, this huge plume is a geyser blowing ionized water out continuously.

3. Rings

Many people known Saturn not because of Titan or Enceladus but because of Saturn’s Magnificent Rings. Saturn has a total of 7 section of rings. Its inner rings makes are made out of dust and ice. The Casinni Division is the dividing section between the A and B rings. This truly is a wonder of the Solar System.

4. Enceladus - Tiger Stripes

Enceladus is not only famous for its plumes, but also for its unique surface. Tidal forces causes this natural satelite to bulge in and out causing it to ‘heat’ up, this energy has no where to go so the only way it could ‘use-up’ this energy is to use it. This is what caused the Tiger Stripes.

5. Cassini Division

There is wide gap between the A and B Rings. This is called the Cassini Division. This is a huge 4,800 kilometre gap mainly consisting of dust and ice.
