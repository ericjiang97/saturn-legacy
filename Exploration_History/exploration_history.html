Pioneer 11(NASA)

Pioneer 11 flew over Saturn on 1979, becoming the first satellite to visit Saturn. It was originally launched to study and investigate asteroid belt activity between Saturn and Jupiter. However, the images it took of Saturn were relatively low quality, in comparison to later pictures taken of the planet.

Voyager 1 (NASA)

Voyager 1 arrived at Saturn in 1980, sending back high quality pictures of the planet. It used Saturn’s gravity to propel itself towards Uranus.

Cassini-Huygens Mission (ESA-NASA)

On 1 July 2004, the Cassini-Huygens spacecraft went into Saturn’s orbit, achieving flybys of Phoebe and Titan. It orbited the planet about 74 times in the space of 4 years, discovering 4 moons and a planetary ring in the process.
